Tribute to Bhagat Singh Ji



About Shaheed Bhagat Singh - A True Hero Of India

Shaheed Bhagat Singh (1907–1931) was one of India’s greatest freedom fighters, remembered for his unmatched courage and sacrifice. Born in Punjab, he grew up with a deep love for the nation and dedicated his life to ending British rule. He became a revolutionary leader in the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and believed that true independence required bravery and sacrifice. From avenging Lala Lajpat Rai’s death to throwing bombs in the Central Assembly to “make the deaf hear,” Bhagat Singh fearlessly fought against injustice. On 23 March 1931, at just 23 years of age, Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were executed by the British. His sacrifice inspired millions, and his slogan “Inquilab Zindabad” still echoes in every Indian heart.

Achievements

  • Joined the freedom struggle at a young age – inspired by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and national leaders.
  • Co-founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha (1926) – to spread awareness among youth against British rule.
  • Member of Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) – worked for complete independence with revolutionary ideas.
  • Avenged Lala Lajpat Rai’s death (1928) – by planning the killing of British officer Saunders.
  • Protested through Assembly Bombing (1929) – threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly to "make the deaf hear," and courted arrest fearlessly
  • Hunger Strike in Jail (1929) – protested against inhumane treatment of political prisoners, gaining widespread support.
